Utilities and structure

Propane (LP) — two drops

East wall: Gas stub-out on 55" house wall → under deck → Blackstone 25K BTU + Napoleon IR 10K BTU = 35K BTU total. Much simpler than PRO 825 Pivot (was 200K BTU).

West wall: Separate LP drop → Coyote C3PBLP power burner 60K BTU.

Total kitchen LP demand: 95K BTU. Down from 200K in PRO 825 Pivot. Traeger is electric (pellet), induction is electric.

Water / drain

Tie into indoor kitchen through west wall at window center. Hot/cold supply + drain for Ruvati sink. Scotsman ice maker tees off cold water line. Gravity drain for ice maker routes to sink drain. Hose bib on bar peninsula east end — tap from nearest outdoor hose bib or dedicated line.

Electrical

Traeger Timberline: dedicated 120V GFCI. Traeger induction cooktop: dedicated 120V (separate circuit). Blackstone 28" XL: 120V for ignition + LEDs. Napoleon IR sear (matte black): 110V for SafetyGlow knobs (+ power supply kit N370-1091-SER for matte black series). KB 36": dedicated 20A. KB 24": dedicated 20A. Scotsman ice maker: dedicated 15A. SunBrite TV: outlet behind mount. GFCI outlets at each work zone per code. Coyote PB: 120V for ignition. LED strip lighting: GFCI shared circuit.

Structure / framing

Treated lumber framing (not steel studs) — insulating jackets required for gas appliances. Exception: Traeger bay must be non-combustible (steel stud + cement board for that section, or 18" clearance to combustibles per Traeger contractor guide). 25" frame depth, 40" counter height, 42" bar height. Granite or porcelain countertop.

Post position

Structural post at SW corner of east wall counter, 26" inset from east deck edge. Through-bolted to deck stringers. 133" bar peninsula butts into post from south side and runs east. Cooking run counter uninterrupted for full 130".

Deck loading

Traeger Timberline: 252 lbs. Blackstone 28" XL: ~150 lbs. KB coolers: ~335 lbs loaded combined. Blocking under all bottom track runs. Sister joists under fridge wall (heaviest zone). Similar to PRO 825 Pivot assessment.

Key changes from PRO 825 Pivot ($28,833):

• PRO 825 Napoleon grill → Traeger Timberline (pellet grill + induction cooktop)
• Blackstone 36" → Blackstone 28" XL (saves 8" width, same premium features)
• Coyote C3PBLP power burner moved from cooking run to window wall (over dog door)
• Napoleon 700 Series 32" grill → Napoleon matte black BIB10IR sear station at south end of cooking run (bar handoff position)
• No appliance in fridge overlap — just counter space
• Coyote CTEP teppanyaki griddle added for smoke-free cooking on power burner
• LP demand drops from 200K BTU to 95K BTU (split across two walls)
• Cooking run maximized with 7.5" gaps between all appliances for plating and heat separation
• No GMG smoker in layout (freestanding elsewhere or removed)
