Standing on deck, looking west toward the house. Left = 45" wall corner (south). Right = 108" wall corner (north).

Construction notes — 130" Window Wall:

Frame: 3-5/8" 20-gauge steel studs, 25" deep (front face to back face) × 40" tall × 130" long. Bottom track screwed to deck boards at 16" o.c., aligned tight against the house wall. Back studs may bear directly on the house siding/sheathing — add a moisture barrier (peel-and-stick or metal flashing) between the steel track and house wall to prevent trapped moisture. Studs at 16" o.c. with double studs flanking each appliance cutout opening.

Sheathing: 1/2" cement board (Hardie backer or equivalent) on all exposed faces — front, sides, and top of frame. Fasten with #8 cement board screws at 8" o.c. into studs. Top sheathing provides the substrate for granite countertop.

Countertop: Granite slab, 1.5" front overhang (26.5" total visible depth). Templated after framing is complete. Cutouts for: sink (29"W × 21"D drop-in), trash pull-out (19.875"W opening), ice maker (15"W built-in), and BBQGuys drawer (29"W × 14.5"H cutout in upper section above dog door).

Cladding: Stacked stone veneer over cement board. Apply scratch coat + thinset mortar. Stone adds ~1-1.5" to wall thickness — account for this when templating granite overhang.

Cutout framing details:
• Trash pull-out (BLZ-TREC-DRW-H): Rough opening 19.875"W × 26.38"H × 24.13"D. Double studs on each side, steel angle header across top.
• Sink (Ruvati RVH8030): 30"W × 22"D drop-in — cutout is in the granite only (29"W × 21"D), no frame opening needed. Reinforce countertop substrate with additional cross-bracing below the cutout to support the filled sink weight (~80 lbs water + sink).
• Ice maker (BLZ-ICEMKR-50GR): 15"W built-in cutout. Front-venting — no rear clearance needed, but leave the back open for water line and drain access. Dedicated 15A circuit.
• BBQGuys drawer (BBQ-DR3015): Cutout 29"W × 14.5"H in the upper portion of the frame above the dog door. Frame a steel header at the bottom of this opening to support the drawer weight. The dog door zone below remains open (no framing below 16" from countertop on the 45" wall side).

Plumbing penetrations: Core through the house wall directly behind the sink location (centered on 79" window). Hot supply, cold supply, and drain. Ice maker tees off the cold water line — short run along the inside of the frame cavity. Gravity drain for ice maker routes to sink drain line.

Electrical: GFCI outlet near sink (on the 130" wall face, not behind the window). Dedicated 15A circuit for ice maker. Route wiring through frame cavity from house.

Construction notes — 130" Cooking Run:

Frame: 3-5/8" 20-gauge steel studs, 25" deep × 40" tall × 130" long. This wall faces the open deck edge (east) — it is entirely freestanding with no house wall backing. Bottom track screwed to deck boards at 16" o.c. Add diagonal steel bracing inside the frame cavity at each end and at midpoint for lateral stability, since there is no wall to brace against. The north end ties into (or butts against) the 108" fridge wall corner. The south end transitions into the 133" bar peninsula post-and-rail system.

Sheathing: 1/2" cement board on all four faces (front, back, top, both ends). The back face (yard side) is fully visible and gets stone veneer. The front face (kitchen side) is where all appliance cutouts and access doors are located.

Countertop: Granite slab, 1.5" front overhang (26.5" total visible depth). This is the longest single counter run — may require a seam depending on slab availability. Cutouts for: grill (23.25"W × 21.25"D), griddle (28.125"W × 24.375"D), power burner (14"W × 19.5"D), and smoker landing (GMG sits on the counter surface, no cutout — just a flat area).

Cladding: Stacked stone veneer on back face (yard-facing) and both end returns. Front face (kitchen-facing) gets stone veneer between and around the access door/drawer openings. Access doors and drawers are flush-mounted into the stone — leave rough openings per specs below.

Heat shields: The grill, griddle, and power burner all generate significant heat. Line the interior cavity walls behind and below each gas appliance cutout with an additional layer of 1/2" cement board or 26-gauge sheet metal. This protects the steel studs from radiant heat. Ensure no combustible material (stone veneer adhesive, wood blocking) is within the clearance zones specified in each appliance installation manual. Minimum clearances: check Blaze manuals — typically 1-2" to non-combustible surfaces on sides and rear.

Gas appliance cutout framing:
• Grill (BLZ-3BCM-LP): Cutout 23.25"W × 21.25"D × 8.5"H below counter surface. Double studs on each side, steel angle or track as header if needed. LP hookup point with individual shutoff valve inside the cavity — accessible through the Blaze 25" double door below.
• Griddle (BLZ-GRIDDLE-LTE-LP): Cutout 28.125"W × 24.375"D × 12"H — this is the deepest appliance and drives the 25" counter depth requirement. Double studs flanking, reinforce with horizontal blocking at cutout base. LP hookup with shutoff. Grease management system drains to a collection tray — ensure the tray is accessible through the Blaze 32" double door below.
• Power burner (BLZ-PROPB-LP): Cutout 14"W × 19.5"D × 9.75"H. Smaller opening but 60,000 BTU output — prioritize heat shielding in this zone. LP hookup with shutoff, accessible through the Blaze 18" vertical door below.
• GMG Smoker: No cutout — sits on the counter surface at the north end (fridge wall corner). Ensure the granite and frame can support the smoker weight. The fridge wall overlaps this zone, so no access doors below.

Below-counter access door/drawer framing:
• Blaze 25" Double Door (BLZ-AD25-R-SC): Rough opening 24.5"W × 17.25"H below the grill zone. Frame with steel track top and bottom, double studs on sides.
• Blaze 32" Double Door (BLZ-AD32-R-SC): Rough opening 31"W × 17.25"H below the griddle zone. Includes built-in paper towel holder.
• Blaze 18" Vertical Door (BLZ-SV-1420-R-SC): Rough opening 17"W × 20.5"H below the power burner zone.
• Blaze 16" Triple Drawer (BLZ-DRW3-R-LT): Rough opening 14.5"W × 20.75"H at the plating area (south end, near bar transition). Open storage below the drawer — no framing needed in the lower portion.
• Filler panel: ~8" stainless steel filler between the triple drawer and the bar transition post.

Gas line routing: LP gas enters the frame cavity at the north end (from the 108" fridge wall, routed under deck from the 55" wall stub-out). Run a manifold or tee inside the cavity with individual branch lines and shutoff valves to each appliance: smoker → grill → griddle → power burner (north to south). Use black iron pipe or CSST per local code. Total demand: ~138,000 BTU — verify the supply line diameter supports this (typically 3/4" minimum for this BTU load at the run lengths involved).

Electrical: GFCI outlet(s) along the cooking run for accessories (rotisserie motor, electric starter, task lighting). Route from house panel through the 108" wall cavity and into this frame.

Construction Cross-Section — Looking Down the Bar

Slice through one steel post. Guest/yard side (left) — Kitchen/house side (right). Post justified to kitchen edge for max guest legroom.

Construction summary: The bar peninsula is supported by three 3" x 3" steel square tube posts (left end, center at ~66", right end), each justified to the kitchen edge of the counter to maximize guest-side legroom (~22" clear). Each post has a welded 6" x 6" x 1/4" base plate, through-bolted to a deck stringer using two 1/2" stainless steel bolts with fender washers and nylock nuts tightened from underneath (direct stringer access required). A steel C-channel or angle iron top rail is welded across all three post tops, running the full 133" length, and the 25" deep countertop (concrete or stone) sits on this rail. No stud wall, no bottom track, no infill — guests have full legroom between posts. All fasteners stainless steel. Posts must land on or adjacent to stringers for through-bolt connection.

Frame a clear opening at deck level — approximately 22"W × 20"H minimum — with no bottom track across the opening. The dog door opening interior can be finished with cement board for a clean look or left as framed steel.

Countertop: The granite from the 130" window wall wraps around this corner as a continuous L-shaped slab (or seamed at the corner). No appliance cutouts on this wall — just a flat counter surface above the dog door and drawer zone.

No utilities on this wall: No gas, water, or dedicated electrical on the 45" wall. The weatherproof GFCI outlet is on the adjacent 130" wall face near this corner.

Deck Structural Assessment:

Existing deck specs: 2×10 joists at 16" o.c., running east–west, 8–10 ft beam span.

Capacity: At 8–10 ft span, 2×10s at 16" o.c. can support ~80–90 lbs/sq ft total load. Standard code requires 50 lbs/sq ft (40 live + 10 dead).

Kitchen load estimate: ~75 lbs/sq ft under the 25" counter footprint, based on:
• Steel stud frame + tracks + bracing: ~15–20 lbs/LF
• Cement board sheathing (1/2", both faces): ~15–20 lbs/LF
• Granite countertop (1.25" thick, 26.5" deep): ~35–45 lbs/LF
• Stacked stone veneer: ~25–35 lbs/LF
• Appliances (averaged across zones): ~20–40 lbs/LF
• Total: ~110–160 lbs/LF ÷ 2 ft depth = ~75 lbs/sq ft

Verdict: Within capacity at 8–10 ft span. The 2×10 joists have enough headroom, but the kitchen loads are above standard residential use (75 vs. 50 lbs/sq ft), so reinforcement is smart insurance.

Heaviest zones (highest priority for reinforcement):
• Fridge wall (108"): Kingsbottle KBU56ASD (~185 lbs fully loaded) + KBU55ASD (~150 lbs loaded) = two heavy point loads close together
• Griddle zone (cooking run): Griddle 121 lbs + Blaze 32" door + granite + stone = peak load spot
• Sink filled: 30" × 10" deep sink + water = ~65 lbs water + sink weight

✅ REQUIRED — Blocking (do this):
• Install 2×10 blocking (short cuts) between joists under every steel bottom track location
• This spreads the counter load across adjacent joists instead of concentrating on whichever joist the track fastener hits
• Estimate 15–20 blocks total, a couple hours of work underneath
• Nail or screw into joist faces on each end — use 3" structural screws or 16d nails

✅ RECOMMENDED — Sister joists under fridge wall:
• Sister the 2–3 joists directly under the 108" fridge wall (the two beverage coolers are the heaviest concentrated loads in the entire kitchen)
• Glue (construction adhesive) + bolt a matching 2×10 alongside each existing joist
• Use 1/2" carriage bolts at 24" o.c. or structural screws (GRK or similar)
• Run sisters the full span if possible, or at minimum 4 ft past the counter footprint on each side

❌ NOT NEEDED:
• Mid-span beam — not required at 8–10 ft span
• Full sistering of every joist — overkill for this load profile
• Structural engineer — loads are within standard capacity with blocking; however, consult one if any joists show rot, splits, or sagging during inspection

Pre-framing checklist:
• Get underneath and visually inspect all joists in the kitchen footprint for rot, splits, or sagging
• Verify joist spacing is actually 16" o.c. (measure several bays)
• Confirm beam span (measure ledger to nearest beam)
• Install blocking under all planned bottom track runs
• Sister joists under fridge wall
• While underneath, plan utility routing paths (gas, water, drain, electrical) before blocking restricts access
