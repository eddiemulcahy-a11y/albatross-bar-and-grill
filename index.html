170" Window Wall — Elevation View

Standing on deck, looking west toward the house. Left = 45" wall corner (south). Right = 108" wall corner (north).

Key features: The sink is centered on the 126" sliding window — direct plumbing tie-in to the indoor kitchen. Trash pull-out sits just left of the sink for easy food prep waste disposal (relocated from 45" wall). The ice maker is to the right (toward the 108" wall), sharing the sink's cold water line. The window doubles as a pass-through for food and dishes. The 45" wall (dog door / cabinets) connects on the left, the 108" fridge wall connects on the right.

108" Fridge Wall — Elevation View

Standing on deck, looking north toward the top wall. Left = 170" wall corner (west/house). Right = open deck corner (east).

Key features: The full 108" wall is dedicated to refrigeration (~96" usable after corner transitions). This could be one large undercounter unit, or 2–3 side-by-side units (fridge + beverage center, fridge + freezer drawer, etc.). A wall-mounted TV (~50") is centered on the 55" house wall above the fridge zone — visible from the bar seating area. The gas stub-out is at deck level, centered on the 55" wall — the gas line drops down and routes under the deck to the grill, griddle, power burner, and smoker on the 130" cooking run. The infrared heater timer switch is on the 55" wall at counter height (not shown in elevation).

130" Cooking Run — Elevation View

Standing inside the kitchen, looking east toward the open deck edge. Left = smoker corner (north). Right = bar transition (south).

Key features: This is the main cooking zone. The GMG smoker sits at the corner near the support post. Then you have the grill, griddle, and power burner all built into the counter surface with storage cabinets below. Gas is piped in under the deck from the 55" wall stub-out — no tank storage needed. Each appliance has a gas hookup point (marked "G") with individual shutoffs. A plating counter provides landing space before the bar transition.

133" Bar Peninsula — Elevation View

Standing in the yard (guest side), looking north at the bar. Left = 130" cooking run transition. Right = end of peninsula.

Key features: Bar counter at 42" with a 12" overhang for comfortable knee clearance. Seats 4–5 guests facing the yard. Full-length storage cabinets on the kitchen side underneath. The corner support post is built into the deck structure — it extends from below the deck up through the counter at the cooking run transition. The countertop wraps around (or butts into) the post for a clean, integrated look. No freestanding post needed; it's part of the framing.

45" Wall — Elevation View

Standing on deck, looking south toward the short house wall. Left = 170" wall corner (west). Right = open deck (east).

Key features: Dog door is justified to the right (faces north / fridge wall) with a clear path below the counter. One large cabinet spans the full width above the dog door at counter height. The weatherproof outlet is on the adjacent 170" wall, not on this wall. Trash pull-out relocated to the 170" window wall near the sink. This wall connects the 170" window counter to the open deck space.

Utilities & Structure

Water / Drain

Tie into indoor kitchen plumbing through the 170" wall at the window center. Core through the wall directly behind the outdoor sink. Hot and cold supply + drain. Ice maker tees off the same cold water line (short run up the 170" wall from the sink).

Gas

Existing stub-out on 55" top wall. Route under deck to: GMG smoker (corner), grill, griddle, and power burner (130" edge). Individual shutoffs at each appliance. Black iron or CSST per code.

Electrical

Existing outlet on 55" wall. Need additional circuits: dedicated 20A for fridge (108" wall), 20A for ice maker (170" wall near sink), GFCI outlets at each zone. Wall-mounted TV on 55" wall needs a dedicated outlet behind the mount (consider in-wall power kit to hide cords). Run under deck from house.

Structure

Counter wraps around support post at corner. Steel stud framing + cement board recommended for deck weight. Verify joist capacity under grill zone. Counter depth: 26–28". Full 170" left wall counter is the longest run — ensure ledger attachment is solid.

Roof Cover

~10 ft overhang covers sink/prep wall, fridge wall, and corner. Grill/griddle at or near edge of cover. Bar peninsula mostly uncovered — consider awning or umbrellas.

Dog Door

On 45" wall — cabinet design needs cutout or pass-through to keep it functional.
