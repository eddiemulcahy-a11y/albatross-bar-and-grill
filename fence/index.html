Fence layout — top-down view

Yard perimeter fence, 1-for-1 replacement of existing 30 posts. Three gates: dock gate on the north, west gate and east gate at the south corners where the side sidewalks enter the yard. Hog wire panels in cedar frame. F1/F4 use alternating 8×8/4×4 post pattern with 2×8 cedar fascia face-mounted on outside (top flush with 8' post tops, bottom at 7'-4.5"). Foundations scope — complete reference

Phase A (Apr 27 – May 2) and Phase D (May 16 – 20). All foundation work to meet 2018 IRC adopted by Island County, WAC 246-359-180 concrete spec (≥2,000 psi), and standard PNW residential fence post practice (Whidbey frost line ~12"; SDC D1 seismic zone, no special detailing required for fences ≤7 ft per IRC R105.2 exemption).

Phase A — Demo & foundations (Apr 27 – May 2)
1. Existing fence demoRemove all existing fencing and 30 posts (22 perimeter + 8 inner). Haul off debris. Confirm with Eddie before pulling any post that's structurally tied to neighbor's fence or shared.
2. New post foundations30 new sono-tube footings: 10 × 8×8 post locations (4 yard corners + 4 F1/F4 alternating middles + 2 dock-flank), 12 × 4×4 perimeter intermediates (3 each on F1, F2, F3, F4), and 8 × 4×4 inner-fence posts (4 per side × 2 sides). 1-for-1 replacement of existing post locations.
Sono tube spec — 8×8 posts12" diameter sono tubes, 3 ft depth below grade (well below 12" frost line). All 10 × 8×8 posts (line, corner, and dock-flank) set to the same depth for consistent foundation work.
Sono tube spec — 4×4 posts10" diameter sono tubes, 3 ft depth below grade. (~3× post width for hole diameter.) All 20 × 4×4 posts (12 perimeter + 8 inner) set to the same depth as the 8×8s for consistent foundation work.
ConcreteMin 2,000 psi compressive strength (per WAC 246-359-180). Standard 60-lb Quikrete fast-set or equivalent (cured 2,500–4,000 psi). Approx 2 bags per 8×8 footing, 1 bag per 4×4 footing ≈ 40 bags total (60 lb each: 10 × 8×8 × 2 bags + 20 × 4×4 × 1 bag = 20 + 20 = 40). Crown concrete 1–2" above grade, slope away from post for drainage.
Drainage base6" of 3/4" angular crushed stone at the bottom of each hole before pouring concrete. Improves drainage and post longevity.
Post-base bracketsSimpson ABA66 / ABU66 standoff post bases (or equivalent) cast into top of each footing, set plumb. Standoff design keeps post end above grade — critical for PNW rot resistance. Confirm bracket size matches actual post dimensions before pour.
3. Trench (perimeter + inner fences)1' deep × 6"–8" wide trench dug along: (a) the full outside perimeter of all 4 fence sections (F1, F2, F3, F4) and (b) along both inner fences from the south of the west/east gates to the house. Total length ≈ 176 ft (140 ft perimeter + ~36 ft inner). Trench follows the fence line on the yard-exterior side. Trench is left open for hog wire install during Phase C; backfilled in Phase D.
Phase D — Backfill & grade (May 16 – 20)
4. Trench backfillAfter the build crew installs the buried 2"×2" hog wire into the trench during Phase C, backfill with native soil, tamping in 6" lifts. Restore grade flush with surrounding yard.
5. Final gradeFinal grade pass around all post bases. Ensure no standing water against any post. Yard cleanup & haul-off.

Build sequence (Phase C, May 9 – 16)
1. Set posts (May 9 – 10)30 cedar posts (22 perimeter + 8 inner) into Simpson ABA66/ABU66 standoff brackets cast in Phase A. Plumb in two planes, brace, fasten with HDG structural screws per Simpson spec.
2. Per-panel framing (May 10 – 13)24 panels (20 perimeter + 4 inner) · 1×4 cedar top rail on its side + continuous 2×4 cedar cap on top of 4×4 intermediates + 1×4 mid-rail sandwich + 1×2 side trim (full panel height, broken at mid-rail).
3. Hog wire install (May 11 – 13, parallel)4×4 mesh in top section. 2×2 mesh in bottom section + drop the buried 12" into the open perimeter trench (vertical, no slack). Stretch tight, fasten with HDG fence staples behind side trim.
4. Fascia install (May 13 – 14)2×8 cedar fascia face-mounted on outside of F1/F4 8×8 posts (and across the 4×4 intermediates between them). Top edge flush with 8×8 post tops at 8'-0"; bottom edge at 7'-4.5". F2/F3 has no fascia (dock arbor on dock-flank ends). Stainless or HDG screws into 8×8 post centers; intermediate fastening into 4×4 posts where alignment allows.
5. Dock arbor (May 14 – 16)3-layer cedar assembly on top of F2/F3 dock-flank 8×8s. Through-bolt or HDG structural screws between layers and into post tops; pre-drill cedar.
Hand-off for backfill (May 16)Walk perimeter together. All buried 2×2 wire dropped into open trench, vertical, no slack. Trench ready for Phase D backfill.
